# [Chapter 10] Executive Synthesis: State Profiles

## 1. The Strategy: A Phased Phased Rollout
"Where should we invest?" Our analysis identifies a three-phase investment strategy.

## 2. Phase 1: The Premium Winners (Tier 1)
**Karnataka, Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Delhi.**
They possess the "Perfect Storm": high urban EV demand, strong state-level subsidies, and solid base IRRs (20-22%).

### 🧠 The Delhi Nuance (High Yield, High Risk)
Delhi presents the highest raw demand volume (projected >150k EVs by 2030) and the most lucrative state subsidies (₹30,000/EV). However, it is **"Critical"** on the grid. 
- **Recommendation:** Proceed in Delhi ONLY if paired with **BESS (Battery Energy Storage Systems)** to load-shift and avoid grid reinforcement fines.

### 🧠 The Telangana Efficiency Case
Telangana actually has the **highest pure financial return (IRR of 23.2%)** due to exceptional solar resources combined with high grid tariffs. However, Delhi still wins on pure **Demand Volume**.

## 3. Phase 2: The Sun Belt Strategy
**Rajasthan and Andhra Pradesh.**
Rajasthan has the best sunlight in India (Highest GHI) but currently lacks enough urban density to hit Tier 1.
- **Recommendation:** Build utility-scale solar farms here and **wheel the power** to high-demand corridors like Delhi or Mumbai rather than building immediate urban charging hubs.

## 4. Phase 3: The Scale Markets
**Uttar Pradesh (UP) and West Bengal.**
These have massive populations but lower current penetration and lower GDP per capita. They are long-term growth plays where government **Viability Gap Funding (VGF)** will be necessary to hit the 15% IRR hurdle.

## 5. Tier 4: The Capital Preservation zone
**Bihar and Jharkhand.**
These states exhibit low demand, low subsidies, and weak grids. Furthermore, their P10 (worst-case) Monte Carlo returns fall below our 10% discount rate hurdle. 
- **Recommendation:** Defer all investment in these regions until hardware costs normalize post-2030.
