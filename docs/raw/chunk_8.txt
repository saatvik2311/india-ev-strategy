5. Interview Cheat Sheet: Anticipated Questions & Answers
Q: Why did you use Monte Carlo simulations? A: "In infrastructure projects lasting 20 years, assuming a single fixed price for electricity or construction is dangerous. I used Monte Carlo to simulate 1,000 different futures. It allows me to tell an investor not just 'You will make 21%,' but 'I am 90% confident your return will not fall below 16%.' It changes the conversation from blind guessing to quantified risk."
Q: How did you handle missing or messy data? A: "In the demand forecasting, some smaller states had almost zero early EV adoption, which breaks log-linear regression. I wrote a fail-safe in the code (if valid.sum() < 2) that defaults to a conservative 35% CAGR fallback if there isn't enough historical data to fit a curve."
Q: What is MCDA and why not just rank by Profit (IRR)? A: "Because highest profit doesn't mean highest viability. A state might have a 25% IRR on paper, but if its power grid is collapsing (poor DISCOM health) or the government bans EV subsidies tomorrow, the project fails. MCDA (Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis) mathematically balances profit with operational reality."
Q: What was the hardest part of building this? A: "Building the custom Bisection Algorithm to calculate IRR from scratch in Python, rather than relying on a black-box library. It required creating a cash flow array mapping 20 years of CAPEX, OPEX, degradation (solar panels lose 0.5% efficiency a year), and green energy certificates, then mathematically finding the exact discount rate where Net Present Value equals zero."
Q: If you had 2 more weeks to work on this, what would you add? A: (Refer to the idea.md future enhancements): "I would integrate Geospatial mapping (GIS). Right now it's state-by-state. I'd want to use the Folium library to map highway corridors (like Delhi-Mumbai) because range anxiety on highways is where the biggest charging gaps exist."
